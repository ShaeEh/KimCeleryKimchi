What is Celery Kimchi

Celery kimchi is a creative and refreshing twist on the classic Korean dish. While traditional kimchi is typically made with napa cabbage, this version uses celery as the main ingredient, offering a unique balance of crunchy texture and bold, spicy flavors. The celery absorbs the tangy, fermented flavors of the kimchi brine, creating a crisp and flavorful dish that’s both familiar and exciting.

With the same foundational ingredients as traditional kimchi, such as gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), garlic, ginger, and fermented fish sauce, celery kimchi delivers a refreshing, crunchy bite. It’s perfect as a side dish, topping, or even a snack. This version allows you to enjoy the health benefits and complex flavors of kimchi with a lighter, crunchier twist.

Ideal for those looking to explore classic Korean flavors in a fresh way, celery kimchi adds a delightful crunch and bold flavor to a wide variety of dishes, making it a versatile and exciting addition to any culinary repertoire.

The History of Kimchi and Its Unique Variations

Kimchi, with its rich tradition, has been a staple in Korean cuisine for centuries. While its exact origins are unclear, it is believed to have been introduced during the 7th century. Traditionally made with napa cabbage, kimchi is fermented with chili flakes, garlic, ginger, and fish sauce, but over time, many variations have emerged. Celery kimchi is one such modern twist. Though it isn't part of the traditional recipe, it offers a fresh take that preserves the signature spicy, sour flavor while adding a satisfying crunch. It’s a testament to how food evolves, keeping tradition alive while inviting new ingredients.

Why You Should Try Celery Kimchi

Celery kimchi isn’t just delicious, it's also a powerhouse of health benefits. The fermentation process introduces probiotics that improve gut health and boost digestion. Celery itself is a low-calorie vegetable, rich in fiber, and high in vitamins like Vitamin K, which helps support bone health. Kimchi, in general, is linked to enhanced metabolism and immunity, and the spicy kick from gochugaru can help boost your metabolism. So not only does it taste great, but it's also great for your body!

Spicy Celery Kimchi Recipes

Made for those who can handle spice, offering a bold, fiery twist on the classic dish with a refreshing crunch!

Ingredients
  • 1 bunch of celery (about 4-5 stalks)
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for salting celery)
  • 3-4 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger (grated)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian version)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar (or white vinegar)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon grated onion (optional for a little extra umami)
  • Water (for rinsing celery)
Instructions:
  • Step 1: Prepare the Celery

    • Wash and cut the celery into bite-sized pieces, typically 2-3 inch pieces. If you like, you can also slice them diagonally for a more aesthetic look.

  • Step 2: Salt the Celery

    • In a large bowl, sprinkle the celery with 1 tablespoon of salt. Toss it to evenly coat.

    Let the celery sit for 30-40 minutes. The salt will draw out excess moisture, softening the celery and allowing it to absorb the kimchi flavors better.

    After 30 minutes, rinse the salted celery thoroughly under cold water to remove the excess salt.

    Drain the celery well to avoid too much liquid in your kimchi mixture.

  • Step 3: Make the Kimchi Paste

    • In a separate bowl, mix together the gochugaru (chili flakes), sugar, garlic, ginger, fish sauce (or soy sauce), rice vinegar, sesame oil, and soy sauce.

    Optionally, add the grated onion to the mixture for an extra depth of flavor.

    Stir everything until you have a thick, paste-like consistency. If the paste is too thick, you can add a bit of water, 1 teaspoon at a time, until it’s spreadable.

  • Step 4: Coat the Celery

    • Once the celery is well-drained, add it to the kimchi paste mixture.

    Use your hands (wear gloves if preferred to avoid chili residue) or a spoon to thoroughly coat the celery with the spicy paste. Make sure every piece of celery is covered with the paste.

  • Step 5: Ferment

    • Pack the coated celery into a clean jar or airtight container. Press the celery down gently to release any air bubbles and to make sure the paste sticks well.

    Leave about 1 inch of space at the top of the jar to allow for expansion during fermentation.

    Leave the jar at room temperature for 1-2 days to ferment. The kimchi will start to develop its tangy flavor during this time. After the initial fermentation, you can store it in the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process.

  • Step 6: Serve

    • After fermenting to your liking, your spicy celery kimchi is ready to be served! It’s great as a side dish, in a salad, or even as a topping for rice.

Crunchy Celery Kimchi Recipe

A refreshing, tangy twist on the classic, offering a satisfying crunch without the heat – perfect for those who prefer a milder, yet flavorful experience!

Ingredients:
  • 1 bunch of celery (about 4-5 stalks)
  • 1 tablespoon salt (for salting the celery)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar (or white vinegar)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger (grated)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons grated carrot (optional, for extra color and sweetness)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped green onion (optional for additional flavor)
Instructions:
  • Step 1: Prepare the Celery

    • Wash the celery thoroughly and cut it into 2-3 inch pieces, depending on your preference. You can slice it diagonally for a more aesthetic presentation or keep it in straight cuts for a traditional look.

  • Step 2: Salt the Celery

    • Place the celery pieces in a large bowl and sprinkle them with 1 tablespoon of salt. Toss the celery to coat it evenly.

    Let it sit for 30-40 minutes to allow the salt to draw out moisture from the celery, softening it a little bit and intensifying the flavor.

    After 30 minutes, rinse the celery thoroughly with cold water to remove the excess salt and drain well.

  • Step 3: Make the Kimchi Paste

    • In a separate bowl, combine fish sauce (or soy sauce), rice vinegar, sugar, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, and soy sauce.

    Stir everything together until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is well combined. If you like, you can add a little grated carrot or chopped green onion to enhance the flavor and texture.

  • Step 4: Coat the Celery

    • Add the drained celery to the bowl with the kimchi paste. Mix everything thoroughly to coat the celery evenly with the paste. The paste will add a tangy, savory flavor without any spiciness.

    You can use your hands (wearing gloves if preferred) to gently massage the paste into the celery for better distribution.

  • Step 5: Pack and Ferment

    • Once the celery is well-coated, pack it into a clean jar or airtight container. Press down gently to release any air pockets and make sure the mixture sticks well to the celery.

    Leave about 1 inch of space at the top of the jar to allow for some expansion during fermentation.

    Let the jar sit at room temperature for about 1-2 days. During this time, the celery will ferment and develop a slight tanginess. You can taste it after 1 day to see if it has reached your preferred level of fermentation.

  • Step 6: Store and Serve

    • Once you’re happy with the flavor and tang, transfer the jar to the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation. It’s best served cold.

    The celery kimchi can be eaten as a side dish, on salads, or even as a topping for rice or sandwiches!

Celery Nutrition

  • Low in Calories: Celery is very low in calories, making it a great choice for weight management.
  • High Water Content: Helps with hydration, contributing to overall fluid balance.
  • Rich in Fiber: Supports digestion, promotes satiety, and aids in weight control.
  • Vitamin K: Essential for bone health and plays a role in blood clotting.
  • Potassium: Helps regulate fluid balance, supports heart health, and maintains muscle and nerve function.
  • Folate: Important for DNA synthesis, cell growth, and especially beneficial during pregnancy.
  • Vitamins A and C: Contribute to healthy skin, eyes, and immune function.
  • Low in Carbohydrates: Suitable for low-carb and blood sugar management diets.
  • Antioxidants: Contains flavonoids and phenolic acids that help reduce inflammation and protect cells from oxidative stress.
